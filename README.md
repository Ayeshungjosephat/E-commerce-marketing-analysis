# 📊 E-commerce Marketing Analytics: Performance Optimization Case Study

## 📌 Overview
This project analyzes multi-channel marketing performance for an e-commerce business, with the goal of improving return on ad spend (ROAS), optimizing budget allocation, and identifying growth opportunities.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem
The marketing team is investing across multiple channels (Google Ads, Email, Facebook Ads, Instagram) but lacks clarity on:
- Which channels drive the highest ROI
- Where budget is being underutilized
- How performance changes over time

---

## 🧠 Approach

I built an interactive dashboard to:
- Track revenue, spend, and conversions
- Compare efficiency across channels
- Identify seasonal trends and performance spikes
- Highlight optimization opportunities

---

## 📊 Key Results

- 💰 **$1M Total Revenue**
- 💸 **$434K Marketing Spend**
- 📈 **2.45 Overall ROAS**
- 🛒 **22K Conversions**

---

## 🔍 Insights & Recommendations

### 1. Google Ads is the Primary Revenue Driver
- Highest ROAS (~3.4)
- Contributes ~32% of total revenue  
✅ *Recommendation:* Scale budget cautiously while monitoring diminishing returns

---

### 2. Email Marketing is the Most Efficient Channel
- Strong ROAS (~2.7) with low spend  
✅ *Recommendation:* Increase investment in lifecycle and retention campaigns

---

### 3. Facebook Ads Drives Volume, Not Efficiency
- Moderate ROAS (~2.1)  
✅ *Recommendation:* Optimize targeting and creatives to improve efficiency

---

### 4. Instagram is Underperforming
- Lowest ROAS (~1.6)  
✅ *Recommendation:* Re-evaluate strategy or reallocate budget

---

### 5. Clear Seasonality Trends
- Revenue spikes during specific months  
✅ *Recommendation:* Align campaign spend with peak demand periods
